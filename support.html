WebWander Chrome Extension — Help & Troubleshooting

Need help with WebWander? You're in the right place. Below you'll find answers to common questions and how to get in touch if you need further assistance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I start stumbling?

Click the WebWander icon in your Chrome toolbar and hit the STUMBLE button. You can also press Alt+S from any tab to stumble instantly.

How do I change my interests?

Open the WebWander popup and click "Update interests" at the bottom of the Home tab. This takes you back to the onboarding screen where you can modify your topic selections and adventurousness level.

What does the adventurousness slider do?

It controls the balance between sites that match your interests closely and sites that are outside your usual topics. Low adventurousness = more of what you love. High adventurousness = more variety and surprise.

Why don't I see the floating toolbar?

The toolbar only appears on pages you navigate to by clicking STUMBLE or pressing Alt+S. It does not appear on pages you visit manually. If you stumbled to a site and don't see the toolbar, try reloading the page. Some sites with strict security policies may block the toolbar from appearing.

How do I save a site to a collection?

When the floating toolbar is visible, click the Save button (or press S). A panel will appear where you can choose an existing collection or create a new one. You can also view your saved collections from the popup's Collections tab.

How do I earn badges?

Badges are earned automatically as you use WebWander. Discover sites, save favorites, maintain streaks, and explore different topics to unlock all 14 badges. Check the Badges tab in the popup to see your progress.

How do I delete all my data?

You can either uninstall the extension (which removes all local data) or go to chrome://extensions, find WebWander, click details, and select "Clear data." See our Privacy Policy for full details.

Keyboard Shortcuts

Quick reference

  • Alt+S — Stumble to a new site (works from any tab)
  • Arrow Up (↑) — Like the current site
  • Arrow Down (↓) — Dislike the current site
  • Arrow Right (→) — Stumble to the next site
  • S — Save to a collection

Known Issues

Things we're aware of

  • Some websites with strict Content Security Policies may prevent the floating toolbar from appearing.
  • The toolbar does not appear on Chrome internal pages (chrome://, chrome-extension://, or the Chrome Web Store).
  • If you've just installed the extension, you may need to reload any open tabs for the keyboard shortcut Alt+S to work.
