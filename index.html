Privacy Policy

WebWander Chrome Extension — Last updated: April 6, 2026

WebWander is built with privacy as a core principle. We believe you should be able to discover amazing websites without sacrificing your personal information. This policy explains exactly what data we handle, how we handle it, and how you stay in control.

What Data We Collect

Data stored by the extension

  • Your selected interests — The topics you choose during onboarding (e.g., Science, Design, Tech) are used to personalize your recommendations.
  • Adventurousness setting — A single number (0–100) that controls how varied your recommendations are.
  • Ratings (likes and dislikes) — When you like or dislike a discovered site, that rating is stored so we can improve future suggestions.
  • Browsing history within the extension — URLs of sites you have stumbled upon through WebWander are recorded to avoid showing you the same site twice.
  • Collections — Any named collections of saved sites you create.
  • Usage statistics — Total sites discovered, sites saved, streak days, and topics explored. These are used to calculate badges and streaks within the extension.
  • Anonymous user ID — A randomly generated UUID (not tied to any personal information) used to associate your preferences with our recommendation API.

What We Do NOT Collect

We never collect any of the following

  • Your name, email address, or any personally identifiable information
  • Your general browsing history or activity outside of WebWander
  • Cookies or tracking pixels
  • Data from any websites you visit (other than the URL when you rate a stumbled site)
  • Keystroke data, form inputs, or page content
  • Location data

WebWander does not track you across the web. The content script that displays the floating toolbar only activates on pages you navigate to via the Stumble button, and it does not read or transmit any page content.

How Data Is Stored

Your data is stored in two places:

  • Locally in your browser — Preferences, ratings, history, and collections are stored using Chrome's built-in chrome.storage API. Preferences sync across your Chrome devices via chrome.storage.sync; ratings, history, and collections use chrome.storage.local and stay on your current device.
  • Our recommendation API — Your anonymous user ID, selected interests, adventurousness setting, and site ratings are sent to our API server to generate personalized recommendations. This data is associated only with your random UUID and cannot be linked to your identity.

How to Delete Your Data

You have two options to remove all WebWander data

  • Uninstall the extension — Removing WebWander from Chrome automatically deletes all locally stored data (preferences, ratings, history, collections, and statistics).
  • Clear extension data manually — Go to chrome://extensions, find WebWander, click the details button, and select "Clear data" (under Site settings / Storage). This removes all local data without uninstalling.

Note: Clearing local data does not automatically delete data stored on our API server. If you would like your server-side data removed, please contact us and we will delete all records associated with your anonymous user ID.

Third-Party Services

WebWander communicates with our own API server to fetch site recommendations and sync ratings. We do not share your data with any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services. We do not sell your data to anyone.

Permissions Explained

  • storage — Required to save your preferences, ratings, collections, and statistics locally in your browser.
  • tabs and activeTab — Required to navigate you to discovered websites when you click Stumble.
  • scripting — Required to inject the floating toolbar overlay on pages you stumble upon.
  • host_permissions (<all_urls>) — Required so the toolbar overlay can appear on any website you discover through WebWander. The extension does not access or read content on any other pages.

Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
